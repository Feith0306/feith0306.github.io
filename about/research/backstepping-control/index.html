Adaptive Backstepping Control for Constrained Uncertain Nonlinear Systems

Methodological Framework

Overview

This research line investigates adaptive control for nonlinear systems whose dynamics are uncertain, constrained, or affected by disturbances and input nonlinearities. Instead of treating neural approximation, backstepping, and robustness as separate tools, the work integrates them into closed-loop designs with explicit stability guarantees.

The studies are motivated by control problems in servo systems, switched multi-agent systems, and strict-feedback nonlinear plants. These systems are practically important because they combine nonlinear dynamics, limited actuation, transient constraints, communication or switching effects, and external disturbances.

Research Questions

  • How can constraints be enforced during learning and adaptation? Servo drives and robotic systems often operate under asymmetric state or output constraints. The controller must regulate tracking errors without allowing transient violations.
  • How can backstepping be made implementable for complex systems? Repeated differentiation in traditional backstepping increases analytical and computational burden. Command-filtered and compensation-based designs are used to reduce this difficulty.
  • How can synchronization be guaranteed under switching, delays, and input nonlinearities? Heterogeneous multi-agent systems require controllers that handle mode changes, state delays, signed interactions, and nonlinear actuator behavior.
  • How can robust tracking be formulated against worst-case disturbances? Zero-sum game theory and H∞ analysis are introduced to learn control and disturbance policies under an adversarial interpretation.

Technical Core

  • Adaptive neural approximation: neural networks approximate unknown nonlinear functions so that exact plant dynamics are not required in controller construction.
  • Backstepping-based recursive design: virtual controls and stabilizing functions are constructed step by step for strict-feedback structures and related nonlinear systems.
  • Constraint-aware error transformation: constrained tracking or regulation errors are converted into stabilizable variables, allowing Lyapunov analysis to enforce admissible operation regions.
  • Command filtering and compensation: command filters reduce the explosion of complexity, while compensation terms attenuate filtering errors and input nonlinearities.
  • Game-theoretic robust learning: the control input and disturbance input are treated as opposing decision variables, leading to Actor–Critic–Disturbance learning for H∞ tracking.

Paper-Based Contributions

  • PMSM servo control: adaptive neural design is used to address permanent magnet synchronous motors with asymmetric constraints and input dead-zone effects, targeting accurate tracking without violating operating constraints.
  • Switched multi-agent synchronization: command-filtered adaptive bipartite synchronization is developed for heterogeneous switched multi-agent systems with state delays and input nonlinearities.
  • Robust nonlinear tracking: Actor–Critic–Disturbance learning is combined with zero-sum game formulation to solve H∞ tracking for nonlinear strict-feedback systems under disturbances.

Representative Outcomes

  • All closed-loop signals are kept bounded under the proposed adaptive laws.
  • State or output constraints are respected throughout the control process.
  • Bipartite synchronization is achieved for heterogeneous agents under switching and delayed dynamics.
  • Robust H∞ tracking performance is obtained by learning disturbance-aware control policies.

Application Relevance

This direction provides a theoretical basis for control of servo drives, robotic actuators, networked agents, and disturbed nonlinear systems. It is especially relevant to scenarios where learning-based control must remain safe under constraints and reliable under uncertainty.
