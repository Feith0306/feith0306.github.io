Data-Efficient Composite Actor–Critic Reinforcement Learning

Methodological Framework

Overview

This research line studies Actor–Critic reinforcement learning for multi-player nonzero-sum differential games. The central difficulty is that each player’s value function and policy are coupled through the other players’ actions, leading to coupled Hamilton–Jacobi equations that are hard to solve analytically.

The work aims to improve data efficiency and convergence reliability by combining instantaneous Bellman errors with historical data. This composite-learning viewpoint weakens the dependence on persistent excitation and makes online Nash policy learning more practical for continuous-time nonlinear systems.

Research Questions

  • How can Nash policies be learned without exact system knowledge? Actor–Critic structures are used to approximate value functions and decentralized policies for each player.
  • How can the restrictive persistence-of-excitation condition be relaxed? Historical data and composite Bellman errors are introduced to replace pure reliance on current online excitation.
  • How can coupled learning errors be controlled? The critic and actor updates of different players interact through the coupled cost functions and dynamics, requiring a joint stability analysis.
  • How can recorded data be made useful rather than merely stored? Data must be selected, verified, and incorporated into the update law so that it contributes to excitation and convergence.

Technical Core

  • Composite Bellman error: the learning error combines instantaneous information with stored historical samples, producing a richer update signal for the Critic.
  • Finite or sufficient excitation: excitation is characterized over recorded data rather than requiring persistent excitation along the entire trajectory.
  • Multi-player Actor–Critic learning: each player maintains a Critic for value-function approximation and an Actor for policy approximation under coupled game costs.
  • Historical data reuse: recorded data are used to strengthen parameter learning, reduce online probing burden, and improve sample efficiency.
  • Lyapunov convergence analysis: stability and convergence properties are analyzed for the closed-loop system and neural-network weight errors.

Paper-Based Contributions

  • Nonzero-sum game formulation: the control problem is modeled as a multi-player differential game where each player minimizes an individual cost coupled with the system state and other players’ policies.
  • Composite critic learning: instantaneous and recorded Bellman errors are integrated to improve critic convergence under weaker excitation requirements.
  • Actor policy improvement: learned critic information is used to update each player’s Actor network toward an approximate Nash equilibrium policy.
  • Data-efficient online implementation: historical data buffers and excitation conditions are used to reduce dependence on continuous exploration signals.

Representative Outcomes

  • Persistence-of-excitation assumptions are relaxed through finite or sufficient excitation over recorded data.
  • Critic and Actor weight errors are driven to bounded neighborhoods under the proposed update laws.
  • Approximate Nash equilibrium policies are learned for multi-player differential games.
  • The closed-loop system remains stable during online learning.
  • Online data requirements are reduced by reusing informative historical samples.

Application Relevance

This direction supports learning-based decision-making for multi-agent autonomous systems, intelligent transportation, cooperative robotics, and networked control problems where agents pursue individual objectives while sharing coupled dynamics.
