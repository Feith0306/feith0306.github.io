Research

This page summarizes three research directions. Each item links to a detailed second-level page.

Adaptive Backstepping Control for Constrained Uncertain Nonlinear Systems

This research focuses on adaptive neural and backstepping control for uncertain nonlinear systems with constraints, delays, input nonlinearities, and external disturbances. The main objective is to guarantee stability, constraint satisfaction, synchronization, and robust tracking performance.

Read more

Projected Self-Triggered Reinforcement Learning for Differential Games

This research develops self-triggered reinforcement learning methods for zero-sum and nonzero-sum differential games. Projection operators, Actor-Critic learning, disturbance learning, fuzzy approximation, and experience replay are integrated to reduce sampling while preserving boundedness and stability.

Read more

Data-Efficient Composite Actor-Critic Reinforcement Learning

This research studies composite Actor-Critic learning for multi-player nonzero-sum differential games. It uses historical data and finite/sufficient excitation to relax persistence of excitation requirements and improve policy learning efficiency.
