Tianhao Fei / Feith Publications Research Work

My research focuses on intelligent control and reinforcement learning for complex nonlinear systems. The three directions below are organized by :

  • adaptive backstepping for constrained uncertain systems;
  • projected self-triggered reinforcement learning for differential games;
  • data-efficient composite actor–Critic reinforcement learning.

Research Map

The common objective is to design learning-based controllers that can be analyzed rigorously and implemented under uncertainty, disturbances, constraints, limited sensing, intermittent feedback, and insufficient excitation.

Adaptive Backstepping Control for Complex Nonlinear Systems

This direction contains three studies on constrained PMSM control, bipartite synchronization of switched heterogeneous multi-agent systems, and robust H∞ tracking for strict-feedback systems. The main techniques include adaptive neural approximation, Nussbaum-type compensation, barrier Lyapunov functions, command-filtered backstepping, Lyapunov-Krasovskii analysis, zero-sum-game reformulation, and Actor-Critic-Disturbance learning.

Projected Self-Triggered Reinforcement Learning for Differential Games

This direction contains two studies on self-triggered H∞ learning for zero-sum games and self-triggered fuzzy reinforcement learning for multi-player nonzero-sum games. The main focus is to remove continuous triggering-condition monitoring while preserving bounded learning dynamics, Zeno-free execution, and game-theoretic stability.

Data-Efficient Composite Actor–Critic Reinforcement Learning

This direction studies online Nash equilibrium learning under finite excitation. It replaces restrictive persistence-of-excitation requirements and probing-noise injection with filtered historical information, composite critic errors, and an online-verifiable excitation condition.
