Projected Self-Triggered Reinforcement Learning for Differential Games

Overview

This research focuses on self-triggered reinforcement learning for differential games, aiming to reduce communication and sampling burden without continuous state monitoring.

Key Problems

  • Avoiding continuous monitoring in online reinforcement learning.
  • Maintaining adaptive weight boundedness during learning.
  • Approximating Nash or saddle-point policies under intermittent updates.
  • Excluding Zeno behavior.

Methodology

  • Self-triggered mechanism.
  • Projected Actor-Critic learning.
  • Actor-Critic-Disturbance learning.
  • Fuzzy logic approximation.
  • Experience replay.
  • Lyapunov-based stability analysis.

Representative Outcomes

  • Reduced triggering frequency.
  • Bounded learning errors.
  • Zeno-free execution.
  • Stable approximate Nash or robust game-theoretic control policies.
